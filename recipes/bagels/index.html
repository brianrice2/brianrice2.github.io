Bagels

Day 1

Ingredients

  • 530g lukewarm water (105-110º)
  • 2 Tbsp barley malt syrup or molasses
  • 885g bread flour
  • 7g yeast
  • 17g salt
  • Neutral oil

Preparation

Mixing and Kneading

Mix the molasses into the water, and the yeast if you're using active dry yeast. Let it proof if so.

Mix the other ingredients in a large bowl. Add the wet solution, and mix with a spatula. Once it's a shaggy ball, cover and let rest for 20 minutes.

Knead the ball in the bowl several times (press-and-fold style), then turn it out onto the counter and knead for 15-20 more minutes, until it's stiff but very smooth and still slightly tacky. Only add flour as needed to prevent sticking.

Form the dough into a ball, dust it lightly with flour, and place it seam-side down into a large bowl. Cover and let rise until doubled, around 1.5-2 hours.

Preshaping

Punch down the dough, then turn it out onto the counter. Cut into 12 pieces of 120-125g each.

Preshape each of the pieces into tight balls: gather all the irregular edges, pinch them together firmly to form a teardrop shape, and place this seam side down. Cup your hand on top of and around the dough like a claw, and draw circles.

Cover the balls with a damp towel and let rest for 5-10 minutes.

Shaping and Proofing

Line two large baking sheets with parchment paper and brush lightly with oil.

One ball at a time, roll it out with your palms into a 9" rope, tapering the ends slightly. Wrap the rope around one hand where your palm and fingers meet, overlapping the ends by 1-2", and roll the dough under your hand back and forth several times to seal. Slip the ring of dough off your hand and stretch it to even out the thickness all the way around - we're shooting for about 4" across. Place it on the baking sheet and move on to the next one.

Finally, cover each baking sheet with plastic wrap and then a damp towel. Transfer to the refrigerator for 4-24 hours.

Day 2

Ingredients

  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 cup barley malt syrup or molasses
  • Toppings of your choice

Preparation

Boiling

About 2 hours before you'd like to serve the bagels, start preheating the oven to 450º. Fill a large Dutch oven halfway with water, with a wire rack and your toppings next to it, for readiness.

To test if the bagels are ready, fill a small bowl with room temperature water. Carefully take one ring of dough from the fridge and transfer it to the bowl - if it floats, it's ready! If not, pull both baking sheets from the fridge, let sit for 30 minutes, and test every 10 minutes after that.

Set the Dutch oven over high heat and bring to a boil. Whisk in the baking soda and molasses - it should look like strong black tea. Reduce the heat to maintain a gentle boil, and skim any foam from the surface.

Take some bagels from the baking sheet and boil for 30 seconds each side. Transfer to the wire rack, and repeat with the rest of the bagels from the first baking sheet.

Baking

Coat the bagels with the toppings of your choice, then place the coated bagels on the empty baking sheet, flat side down.

Bake until the bagels are deeply brown, 20-25 minutes, rotating the baking sheet 180º after 12 minutes.

Repeat this process with the other baking sheet.

Let the bagels cool completely on a wire rack before slicing.
